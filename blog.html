I am currently a PhD student at Institute of Theoretical Physics, Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University. My research interests are modified theories of gravity, black holes and gravitational waves. MORE ABOUT RESEARCH

I completed my B.Tech. (Bachelors) in Computer Science from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Patna where I spent some excellent four years of my life. My Bachelor Thesis supervised by Prof. Jimson Mathew highlighted a novel non-monotonic gradient descent optimizer which aims to reduce the number of divergences while using gradient descent, along with its theoretical and empirical validation.

During my bachelors, I worked as a Research Affiliate at MIT Media Lab to create Brain-Computer Interfaces for real-time prediction in assistive devices. Previously, I was a Software Engineer Intern at Sybill.ai and the Expedia Group. Last to last year, I collaborated with Prof. Jimson Mathew and a research scientist at JCBC, University of Cambridge to work on early diagnosis of Alzheimer’s disease using deep learning methods. Prior to that, I have been a successful Google Summer of Code (GSoC) 2019 student contributing backend code to the Open Source organization Rocket.Chat.

Please feel free to check out my resume and drop me an email if you want to chat with me!
